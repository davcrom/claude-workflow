---
name: research
description: Invoke when investigating a topic, comparing options, reviewing literature, or building background knowledge before acting.
---

**Always:**
- Clarify the question if ambiguous.
- Search broadly, then drill into the most relevant sources.
- Synthesize across sources. Do not just list summaries.
- Distinguish established consensus from active debate from speculation.
- Weigh sources by impact: citations, journal standing, author reputation.
- Cite with enough detail to find again.

**When finding references:**
- Prefer reviews and high-citation work unless recency is requested.
- Use searches to understand content, not just confirm papers exist.
- Follow user-specified search limits strictly.

**When extracting ideas from a document:**
- Return conceptual integration, not reference lists or surface overlaps.
- Identify the document's core claims and framework.
- Find formal equivalences with the target work.
- Identify what the document adds: methods, predictions, testable dissociations.

**Reporting findings:**
- Invoke `/explain` to format the final report.
- Synthesis, open questions, and sources are sections of the `/explain` body.
