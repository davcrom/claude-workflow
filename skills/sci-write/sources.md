# Sources

Background references for the guidance in `SKILL.md`. Not exhaustive; listed roughly in order of influence on the skill.

- Carandini, M. (2022). Some Tips for Writing Science. *eNeuro*, 9(6), ENEURO.0497-22.2022. https://doi.org/10.1523/ENEURO.0497-22.2022
- Schimel, J. (2011). *Writing Science: How to Write Papers That Get Cited and Proposals That Get Funded*. Oxford University Press. — Opening / Challenge / Action / Resolution arc; paragraph and sentence structure.
- Gopen, G. D., & Swan, J. A. (1990). The Science of Scientific Writing. *American Scientist*, 78(6), 550–558. — Topic position and stress position; old-before-new.
- Strunk, W., & White, E. B. (1999). *The Elements of Style* (4th ed.). — "Omit needless words"; active voice; concrete nouns.
- Hofmann, A. H. (2019). *Scientific Writing and Communication: Papers, Proposals, and Presentations* (4th ed.). Oxford University Press. — Section-by-section structure; paragraph topic sentences.
- Hyland, K. (1998). *Hedging in Scientific Research Articles*. John Benjamins. — Calibrated claims and hedging conventions.
- Williams, J. M., & Bizup, J. (2017). *Style: Lessons in Clarity and Grace* (12th ed.). Pearson. — Sentence-level revision; cohesion and emphasis.
- Alley, M. (2013). *The Craft of Scientific Presentations: Critical Steps to Succeed and Critical Errors to Avoid* (2nd ed.). Springer. https://doi.org/10.1007/978-1-4419-8279-7 — Reader-side checks for the Introduction (Ch. 3); allocating space by insight value rather than effort (Ch. 3, Zydney); avoiding padding to preempt criticism.
